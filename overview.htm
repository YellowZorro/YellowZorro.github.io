Overview

We will be exploring the ways in which people learn to love each other through torture and interrogation. Oceania strives to keep everyone the same, and it seems as though our enemies do the same. Torture and interrogation isn't all about making others feel pain. Thinking about pain and avoiding it is often enough to deter citizens from doing independent deeds.

George Orwell's 1984 might have put us in a bad light, but just wait until we show you the rest of the world and their tactics. ISIS in Eurasia, the DPRK in Eastasia, they all roughly correspond to our own government, but we all know Big Brother is superior in every which way.

George Orwell's 1984 follows the struggles of Winston Smith, a shallow man that didn't know his place. He just didn't know where he belonged in the world, and he was driven to hate Big Brother (the nerve of him!). He commited many evil deeds, including, but not limited to, adultery and thoughtcrime. He thought he could revolt against Oceania and Big Brother, but his naivete proved to be his undoing. Those that control the past, control the present, and therefore, the future.

George Orwell uses the theme of interrogation and torture to show how easy it is for us and governments around the world to instill fear in their citizens for the purposes of complete control. All governments use these tactics to some extent in order to keep hold of the powers that they hold over their subjects.
