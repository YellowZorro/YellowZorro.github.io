Conflict

Torture and Interrogation has been the underlying force that causes people to do things that might disagree with their own thoughts. Room 101 and the Ministry of Love are key examples which Winston and other Thought-Criminals go to receive judgement. It is what drives society: The impending doom that cannot be avoided in that society.

North Korea, or the Democratic People’s Republic of Korea, or even Joseon is what they call themselves, is a great example in which torture and interrogation can be used psychologically, and in some cases, too far. The fact that the government is still in power is what forces people to lead these lives, essentially torture while living. The prison system, per se, isn’t really well organized, and the lower branches of the government are conspicuously corrupt. The lack of food, destabilized public systems, not to mention the highly selective elite group that controls the majority. This is all comparable with Ingsoc: and this is only with minimal government control and surveillance. Concentration camps make up the Northern part of the country, and once you go there, you are held there for three generations, or so they say (Demick). Even something like the Ministry of Love and Room 101 exist: Reeducation camps, where prisoners have to meet the daily quota for work, as well as memorizing and revering the Supreme Leaders (Prisons). Though situations might seem desperate, conflict just doesn’t brew within the country though: people don’t feel the need to revolt, as the majority don’t know any better; and the minority, gone. People don’t live in fear in North Korea. Rather, they feel a sense of lacking: hunger, recreation, life in general (Demick). This fuels a minority group to not want more, and nothing to envy from the outside world.

What these minority experience is what makes the majority rule. Some use ethnic cleansing, some concentration camps. Even reeducation camps, which aim to change the victim’s mind to the ones the rulers want counts as torture. Its single purpose is to make the majority the superior, the false into the truth. “How does one man assert his power over another ...? … By making him suffer. Obedience is not enough. Unless he is suffering, how can you know he is obeying your will and not his own?” (Orwell 220) Mental torture is as bad as, if not worse, than classical physical torture.

We can see in the year 2014, however, that ISIS, a minority group by most standards, strike fear into the hearts of those in the Middle East of Eurasia. By committing atrocious acts of submission, ISIS is able to control the masses through fear. By these means, extreme groups are able to become the majority as they take control. The acts that they do are enough to break people, physically and mentally. As one victim of their deeds say, “They have already killed my body. They are now killing my soul.” (Fendel)

Human Rights violations is considered for granted in established countries like ours. However, even countries that are allied with us, or even in the shadows of our country, lie despicable deeds, all for basically asserting dominance over one another. The minority that believe in the future, such as Winston, is eliminated, or they escape. Torture and Interrogation play a key role in all of these societies by putting pressure to those that do not conform to their form of rule. Granted, North Korea does not hold a perfect system such as Ingsoc, but it might be the closest thing we have in this world.
