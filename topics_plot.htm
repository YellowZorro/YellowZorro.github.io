Plot

Torture plays a significant role in the novel, as all the main characters live and act knowing that they will eventually pay the price for their actions. Winston, when he begins his diary, puts a “grain of whitish dust and [deposits] it on the corner of the cover,” not to prevent the Thought Police from finding it, but to at least know if they had found it (Orwell 36). Obviously we found it, but we could tell what he was trying to do, so we went ahead and replaced the dust also. Winston and Julia’s relationship is also indirectly altered by the threat of being sent here, since they try their best to conceal their hidden affair. This causes them to go far out into the country and also make arrangements in the room above Mr. Charrington’s store, although this was ultimately our trap.

Ultimately, the events that occur once Winston becomes our visitor provide the most detailed explanation of the procedures we use at the Ministry of Love. Due to his alleged affiliation with Goldstein and the Brotherhood, Orwell writes in a way to make the Ministry of Love look bad, however if you look carefully at the details, it was Winston that caused the rehabilitation to be so painful. While Winston is undergoing the healing process, O’Brien explains to him how the Ministry operates. O’Brien tells Winston that “‘The Party is not interested in the overt act: the thought is all we care about. We do not merely destroy our enemies, we change them’” (Orwell 319). And here lies the fundamental difference between us, and the governments of Eurasia and Eastasia.

If you look at the situation in other areas of the world, such as a region of Eurasia known as the Middle East, you will find that the situation is not like it is in Oceania, where we have qualified therapists helping thought-criminals one-on-one to help them realign themselves with Big Brother, as shown in the above picture. The government in parts of the Middle East, known as ISIS, use fear to torture their prisoners and ultimately execute them. Recently they have posted videos in which they showed the live beheading of reporters from the United States and an aid worker from Airstrip One. Although we do not wish for you to see these videos, here is a report done by the Ministry of Truth.

There is a rumor that the executioner is in fact from Airstrip One. If so, it is almost certainly Goldstein, for “his first two victims were American journalists. His third was a fellow citizen, British aid worker David Haines” (Bergen). What Big Brother-loving Oceanian would ever draw the blood of another Oceanian? Also, although we are at war with Eastasia, and have always been at war with Eastasia, “the total number of Westerners who have fought in Syria [is] between 2,620 and 2,870” (Bergen). Even without being at war, it is refreshing to see Oceanians fight for what the Party believes is morally right.

Meanwhile, the situation is similar is Eastasia. Although they do not host public executions such as those held by ISIS, the North Koreans hold many of their own citizens in concentration camps, “where inmates are routinely tortured and killed, according to prisoners who have escaped” (Moore). Concentration camps of this scale have not been seen since before the Revolution, when a man, named Adolf Hitler, from a region of Eurasia then known as Germany, sent people of the now-extinct Jewish faith into similar camps. It is tragic that the North Koreans have no respect for human life, using them as bait to try and agitate other governments, such as the Partymen in the United States (Moore).

Surely you now realize that the “torture” Orwell describes in 1984 in no way compares to the atrocities occurring elsewhere. In fact, the Ministry of Love puts effort into reconverting each and every person that passes through the doors of Room 101 back into a sane Party member.
